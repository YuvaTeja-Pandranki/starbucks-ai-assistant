================================================================================
STARBUCKS STORE MANAGER GUIDE
Version 3.5 | Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Applies To: Store Managers and Assistant Store Managers
================================================================================

================================================================================
SECTION 1: DAILY MANAGEMENT RESPONSIBILITIES
================================================================================

1.1 OPENING DUTIES (60 MINUTES BEFORE OPEN)
- Arrive 60 minutes before store opening to complete pre-open checklist.
- Unlock and disable alarm (code in secure manager portal — never share).
- Review the previous day's closing notes left by the closing supervisor.
- Verify cash drawer starting amounts (standard $200 till per register).
- Check temperature logs: refrigerators must be at 38°F or below; freezers at 0°F.
- Confirm all equipment is operational — run a test shot on the Mastrena II.
- Review staffing against the scheduled lineup — arrange coverage if needed.
- Brief the opening team on any product changes, promotions, or customer feedback from yesterday.

1.2 MID-SHIFT MANAGEMENT
- Walk the floor every 30 minutes during peak hours to observe service flow.
- Monitor the mobile order queue — queue length over 15 is a red flag for capacity planning.
- Check customer wait times: intervene if average wait exceeds 5 minutes.
- Monitor waste: excessive waste may indicate preparation errors or overstocking.
- Conduct 10-minute huddle with team at shift change (use the "Connection Meeting" format).

1.3 CLOSING DUTIES
- Ensure closing checklist is 100% complete before locking up.
- Count all tills — any variance over $10 requires incident documentation.
- Set the overnight safe with the day's deposits sealed and logged.
- Complete the nightly food waste log.
- Lock all external doors and re-enable the alarm.
- Leave detailed notes for the opening manager in the handover log.

================================================================================
SECTION 2: WEEKLY REPORTING REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

2.1 LABOR REPORT
Submit the weekly labor efficiency report every Monday by 10 AM via the Store Portal:
- Actual labor hours vs. planned labor hours.
- Overtime hours (must be justified if over budget).
- Turnover incidents (new hires, terminations, resignations in the period).
Target: Labor as a percentage of sales should not exceed 32%.

2.2 WASTE REPORT
Submit waste log summary every Sunday by 11:59 PM:
- Total waste value in dollars.
- Top 3 wasted items and root cause analysis.
- Any food safety-related waste (expiration, temperature breach).
Target: Waste should be under 2% of weekly product cost.

2.3 CUSTOMER FEEDBACK REPORT
Review all customer feedback from the following sources weekly:
- Starbucks app reviews (district manager reviews alongside you).
- Google and Yelp reviews — respond to any negative reviews within 48 hours.
- In-store feedback forms deposited in the feedback box (behind register).
Document recurring themes and share with the team at the weekly huddle.

2.4 SALES AND UPSELL METRICS
The district manager reviews these weekly — be prepared to discuss:
- Total sales vs. prior year same week.
- Average ticket value (target: +$0.50 vs. prior year).
- Top 5 selling items and any significant mix shifts.

================================================================================
SECTION 3: STAFF PERFORMANCE REVIEWS
================================================================================

3.1 REVIEW CADENCE
- New partners: 30-day check-in (informal), 90-day formal review.
- Established partners: Semi-annual formal review (January and July).
- Shift supervisors: Quarterly formal review with development plan.

3.2 PERFORMANCE DIMENSIONS
Reviews assess partners on five dimensions (each scored 1-5):
1. Customer Connection: Warmth, personalization, and complaint resolution.
2. Craft: Beverage quality, speed, and consistency.
3. Safety and Cleanliness: Adherence to food safety and cleaning standards.
4. Teamwork: Communication, reliability, and support of fellow partners.
5. Growth: Initiative, learning new skills, and stepping up in difficult situations.

3.3 DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
All performance conversations must be documented in the HR system (Workday):
- Date of conversation.
- Specific behaviors discussed (positive and developmental).
- Action plan with agreed timelines.
- Partner acknowledgment signature (digital or physical).

3.4 PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLANS
If a partner scores below 3 in two or more dimensions:
- Initiate a 30-day Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) in Workday.
- Hold weekly check-ins during the PIP period.
- Notify the district manager when a PIP is opened.

================================================================================
SECTION 4: BUDGET AND COST CONTROL
================================================================================

4.1 MONTHLY P&L OWNERSHIP
Store managers are responsible for the store's monthly P&L. Key line items:
- Revenue: Compare actuals to plan — flag any variance over 5% immediately.
- COGS: Target 29-31% of revenue. Investigate if above 33%.
- Labor: Target 30-32% of revenue. Overtime should be less than 3% of total labor.
- Supplies: Monitor single-use cup and sleeve costs. Offer lids-on-request if costs spike.

4.2 DISCRETIONARY SPENDING
Managers have discretionary authority for:
- Minor repairs and maintenance: Up to $250 without approval.
- Partner supplies (aprons, name tags): Up to $150 per quarter.
- Customer recovery costs: Up to $100 per incident without escalation.
Anything above these thresholds requires district manager pre-approval.

4.3 ORDERING AND INVENTORY
Weekly product orders are placed in the Sysco and Starbucks Distribution Portal by Wednesday 5 PM for Friday delivery. Over-ordering is as costly as under-ordering — calibrate orders to:
- Current weekly sales velocity (from POS sales mix report).
- Upcoming promotions or seasonal launches.
- Variance from prior week's waste log.

================================================================================
SECTION 5: VENDOR MANAGEMENT AND ORDERING
================================================================================

5.1 PRIMARY VENDORS
- Starbucks Distribution Center: All branded products (syrups, cups, beans, food).
- Sysco Food Services: Supplementary food items and cleaning supplies.
- Aramark: Linen and uniform services (aprons, towels).
- Equipment Services: Mastrena and Vitamix service contracts.

5.2 DELIVERY RECEIVING PROCEDURES
When a delivery arrives:
1. Count all items against the packing slip — do not sign until count is confirmed.
2. Inspect for damaged, dented, or expired products.
3. Reject any refrigerated items that arrived above 41°F (check with probe thermometer).
4. Log the delivery in the Receiving Log with date, supplier, and any discrepancies.
5. Inform the district manager of any shortage over $200 in a single delivery.

5.3 VENDOR DISPUTES
Shortages or damaged goods must be reported within 24 hours of delivery via the
Supplier Portal. Credit memos typically process within 5 business days.

================================================================================
SECTION 6: HEALTH AND SAFETY INSPECTIONS
================================================================================

6.1 HEALTH DEPARTMENT INSPECTIONS
Health inspections are unannounced. When an inspector arrives:
1. Welcome the inspector professionally and provide your manager credentials.
2. Accompany them throughout the inspection — never leave them unescorted.
3. Answer questions honestly and transparently.
4. Do not interrupt an observation — wait for the inspector to complete before clarifying.
5. Request a copy of the inspection report before the inspector leaves.

6.2 CRITICAL VIOLATIONS (IMMEDIATE CLOSURE RISK)
The following require immediate corrective action during the inspection:
- Refrigeration failure (above 41°F for more than 2 hours).
- Evidence of pest activity.
- Handwashing sink blocked, non-functional, or stocked with product.
- Partner not wearing gloves when handling ready-to-eat food.
Notify the district manager immediately if any critical violation is cited.

6.3 SELF-AUDIT SCHEDULE
Conduct internal health and safety audits monthly using the Health Audit Checklist
(available in the Store Operations Toolkit). Score must be above 90/100 to avoid
a mandatory district manager review.

================================================================================
SECTION 7: CRISIS MANAGEMENT PROTOCOLS
================================================================================

7.1 FOOD SAFETY INCIDENT (CUSTOMER ILLNESS REPORT)
If a customer reports illness potentially linked to store products:
1. Take the report seriously — do not dismiss or minimize.
2. Collect: customer name, contact, item consumed, symptoms, time of consumption.
3. Immediately pull and quarantine any remaining product from the same batch.
4. Notify the district manager and the Starbucks Food Safety Hotline: 1-888-XXX-XXXX.
5. Do not speak to media. Direct all inquiries to media@starbucks.com.
6. Cooperate fully with any health department investigation.

7.2 DATA BREACH OR POS SYSTEM COMPROMISE
If there is reason to believe the POS system or customer data has been compromised:
1. Immediately switch to manual order-taking and cash-only transactions.
2. Do not attempt to reset or reboot the POS — preserve the state for IT investigation.
3. Call the Starbucks IT Security Hotline: 1-888-XXX-XXXX.
4. Notify the district manager within 30 minutes.

7.3 ADVERSE WEATHER OR EMERGENCY CLOSURE
In the event of mandatory closure (weather, utility failure, emergency):
1. Post store closure on the Starbucks app via the Store Portal within 15 minutes.
2. Notify all scheduled partners via Teamworks message.
3. Complete the Emergency Closure Form and submit to the district manager.
4. Preserve perishable inventory using dry ice from the emergency supply if power is out.
