SECTION 1: PARTNER STANDARDS AND CODE OF CONDUCT
================================================================================

1.1 THE STARBUCKS MISSION
"To inspire and nurture the human spirit — one person, one cup, and one neighborhood
at a time." Every action taken in the store must reflect this mission.

1.2 CORE VALUES
- Creating a culture of warmth and belonging.
- Acting with courage: challenging the status quo and finding new ways to grow.
- Being present, connecting with transparency, dignity, and respect.
- Delivering the best in all we do, and holding ourselves accountable.

1.3 PROHIBITED CONDUCT
The following behaviors are grounds for immediate termination:
- Theft of product, cash, or property.
- Falsification of time records or waste logs.
- Physical altercation with a customer or fellow partner.
- Reporting to work under the influence of alcohol or controlled substances.
- Sharing customer PII (names, payment data) outside authorized systems.
- Discriminatory or harassing behavior of any kind toward customers or partners.

1.4 PROGRESSIVE DISCIPLINE POLICY
Minor violations follow a three-step process:
Step 1: Verbal coaching documented in partner file.
Step 2: Written warning signed by partner and manager.
Step 3: Final written warning or suspension pending review.
Severe violations (theft, harassment, safety breaches) bypass progressive steps.

SECTION 2: DRESS CODE AND APPEARANCE (GREEN APRON POLICY)
================================================================================

2.1 THE GREEN APRON
The Starbucks green apron is a symbol of the brand and must be worn at all times
while on the floor. Aprons must be:
- Clean and free from visible stains at the start of every shift.
- Returned to the store upon resignation or termination.
- Never worn outside the store during personal errands during breaks.

2.2 CLOTHING REQUIREMENTS
- Solid-color tops (no logos, graphics, or patterns) in black, white, grey, or navy.
- Pants, jeans, or skirts in solid dark colors — no rips, holes, or fraying.
- Closed-toe, slip-resistant shoes are mandatory at all times in the store.
- No open-toe footwear, sandals, or high heels.

2.3 HAIR AND GROOMING
- Hair must be tied back securely when working at bar or food prep areas.
- No excessive or dangling jewelry that could fall into beverages.
- Nail polish is permitted but nails must be kept short and clean.
- Artificial nails are not permitted in food and beverage preparation areas.
- Fragrance (cologne, perfume) should be minimal — strong scents affect beverage aroma.

2.4 VISIBLE TATTOOS AND PIERCINGS
Starbucks respects individual expression:
- Visible tattoos are permitted as long as they are not offensive or explicit.
- Facial piercings are permitted in limited quantity (no more than 2 visible).
- Gauges are permitted but must be covered with flesh-tone plugs during service.

SECTION 3: ATTENDANCE AND PUNCTUALITY RULES
================================================================================

3.1 REPORTING FOR SHIFTS
Partners must arrive and be ready to begin work (apron on, station set up) at their
scheduled shift start time. Arriving 5 minutes early is encouraged.

3.2 ABSENCE NOTIFICATION
If you are unable to attend a scheduled shift:
- Notify the store manager or shift supervisor at least 2 hours before shift start.
- Call the store directly — text messages to personal numbers are not acceptable.
- A manager-approved absence does not count as an occurrence.

3.3 ATTENDANCE POINT SYSTEM
Occurrences accumulate as follows:
- Unexcused absence: 1.0 point
- Late arrival (1-15 minutes): 0.5 points
- Late arrival (over 15 minutes): 1.0 point
- No-call/no-show: 2.0 points
Accumulating 5 points within a rolling 6-month period triggers a formal review.
Accumulating 8 points within 6 months may result in termination.

3.4 SHIFT SWAPPING
Shift swaps must be approved by a manager in Teamworks (the scheduling app) at least
24 hours before the shift. Informal agreements between partners without manager approval
in Teamworks do not constitute an approved swap.

SECTION 4: SAFETY AND FOOD HANDLING PROCEDURES
================================================================================

4.1 HANDWASHING REQUIREMENTS
Partners must wash hands:
- At the start of every shift.
- After handling raw food items.
- After touching face, hair, or mobile phone.
- After handling cash.
- After using the restroom.
- After any cleaning task involving chemicals.
Handwashing must last a minimum of 20 seconds with soap and warm water.

4.2 FOOD TEMPERATURE STANDARDS
- Hot food items must be held at 135°F (57°C) or above.
- Cold food items must be kept at 41°F (5°C) or below.
- Milk opened for steaming must be refrigerated if unused within 15 minutes.
- Dairy products must not sit at room temperature for more than 2 hours.

4.3 FOOD LABELING AND DATING
All food items prepared in-store must be labeled with:
- Item name.
- Preparation date and time.
- Discard date and time (per product shelf-life guidelines).
Unlabeled or expired items must be wasted immediately and logged in the waste tracker.

4.4 ALLERGEN PREVENTION
- Use color-coded utensils (posted in back-of-house) for allergen-free preparation.
- Blenders used for nut-based beverages must be thoroughly cleaned before reuse.
- Partners should wash hands between handling different allergen categories.

SECTION 5: EQUIPMENT OPERATION GUIDELINES
================================================================================

5.1 ESPRESSO MACHINES (MASTRENA II)
- Do not attempt repairs — call the equipment support line: 1-800-XXX-XXXX.
- Backflush the group head at the end of every shift.
- Purge steam wands after every use — do not allow milk to dry in the wand.
- Report any error codes to the shift supervisor immediately.
- Temperature range for espresso: 195-205°F (90-96°C).

5.2 BLENDERS (VITAMIX LEGACY)
- Clean blender jar with hot water and one drop of soap after every blended drink.
- Never fill blender above the MAX line — lid will not seal safely above this level.
- Replace blender jars showing cracks or chips — report to manager for replacement.

5.3 POS SYSTEM
- Never share your POS login PIN with another partner.
- Log out of the POS terminal when stepping away for more than 2 minutes.
- Void transactions using your own PIN — do not use a supervisor's PIN unsupervised.
- Count your till before and after every shift. Report discrepancies over $1 immediately.

SECTION 6: EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
================================================================================

6.1 FIRE EMERGENCY
1. Call 911 immediately if fire is visible or smoke alarm activates.
2. Do not attempt to fight a fire — evacuate all customers and partners first.
3. Use the nearest exit — do not use elevators.
4. Assemble at the designated muster point (posted in back-of-house).
5. Notify the store manager and district manager after emergency services are called.

6.2 MEDICAL EMERGENCY
If a customer or partner requires medical assistance:
1. Call 911 immediately for any life-threatening situation.
2. Do not move an injured person unless they are in immediate danger.
3. Retrieve the first aid kit (located under the bar, near the handwashing sink).
4. Designate one partner to meet and direct emergency services at the entrance.
5. Document the incident in the Incident Report within 24 hours.

6.3 SECURITY INCIDENTS (ROBBERY OR THREAT)
1. Comply with all demands — no product or cash is worth partner or customer safety.
2. Do not attempt to pursue, follow, or photograph the individual.
3. Call 911 after the individual has left the premises.
4. Secure the store and retain all customers as witnesses if safe to do so.
5. Contact the district manager immediately after emergency services.

6.4 IRATE CUSTOMER
If a customer becomes verbally aggressive or threatening:
1. Remain calm and lower your own voice.
2. Immediately involve a shift supervisor.
3. If threats become physical, call 911 and move to safety.
4. Do not confront or block the exit.

SECTION 7: SOCIAL MEDIA POLICY
================================================================================

7.1 PERSONAL SOCIAL MEDIA
Partners are permitted to maintain personal social media accounts. However:
- Do not post photos of the store interior, POS, or back-of-house areas.
- Do not post customer images without explicit consent.
- Do not share confidential product launch information before the public release date.
- Do not claim to represent Starbucks in posts — always clarify personal opinion.

7.2 BRAND CRITICISM
Partners may hold and express personal opinions about Starbucks as an employer,
consistent with NLRA rights. However, sharing trade secrets, operational data,
or confidential financial information is a terminable offense.

7.3 INFLUENCER AND MEDIA REQUESTS
If a media outlet, influencer, or journalist contacts the store or a partner for comment:
- Politely decline and direct them to: media@starbucks.com.
- Do not make statements on behalf of Starbucks under any circumstances.
- Notify the store manager of the contact immediately.
