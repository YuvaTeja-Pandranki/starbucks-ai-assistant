Version 5.0 | Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Applies To: All Baristas and Shift Supervisors

================================================================================
SECTION 1: ESPRESSO STANDARDS
================================================================================

1.1 SHOT FUNDAMENTALS
Starbucks espresso is pulled exclusively on the Mastrena II superautomatic machine.
Each shot must meet the following standards before being used in a beverage:
- Shot volume: 1 oz (single), 2 oz (double — standard for most beverages)
- Pull time: 18-23 seconds per shot (Mastrena auto-calibrated)
- Temperature: 195-205°F at point of extraction
- Color: Rich golden-brown with uniform reddish crema on top

1.2 CREMA STANDARDS
A properly pulled shot will have a visible crema (foam layer) covering the top.
Crema that is pale yellow indicates under-extraction — the shot should be discarded.
Crema that is dark brown and thin indicates over-extraction — discard and recalibrate.
Shots must be used within 10 seconds of pulling — do not let shots sit and oxidize.

1.3 SHOT QUANTITIES BY DRINK SIZE
- Short (8 oz): 1 shot
- Tall (12 oz): 1 shot
- Grande (16 oz): 2 shots
- Venti hot (20 oz): 2 shots
- Venti iced (24 oz): 3 shots
Customers may request additional shots (extra charge applies per additional shot).

1.4 RISTRETTO AND LUNGO
- Ristretto: Shorter pull (15 seconds), less volume, sweeter and more concentrated.
- Lungo: Longer pull (30 seconds), more volume, more bitter notes.
These are available upon customer request but require manual mode on the Mastrena II.

================================================================================
SECTION 2: MILK STEAMING GUIDELINES
================================================================================

2.1 TEMPERATURE STANDARDS
- Standard temperature: 150°F (65°C) for all hot beverages.
- Extra hot: 180°F (82°C) — only when customer explicitly requests it.
- Kids' temperature: 130°F (54°C) — for any children's drinks.
- Cold foam: Steamed at 32-38°F using cold milk in the cold foam blender.
Always use a thermometer for accuracy — never guess temperature by touch alone.

2.2 STEAMING TECHNIQUE
1. Fill the pitcher to just below the spout for small batches (1-2 drinks).
2. Purge the steam wand before immersing in milk.
3. Angle the wand slightly off-center to create a whirlpool motion.
4. Keep the wand tip just below the milk surface during the stretch phase (first 3 sec).
5. Submerge the wand deeper and allow the whirlpool to integrate foam.
6. Stop steaming at 5°F below target — carryover heat will reach target.
7. Purge the steam wand immediately after removing from pitcher.
8. Wipe wand clean with a damp cloth after every purge.

2.3 FOAM RATIOS BY DRINK TYPE
- Latte: Minimal foam (microfoam only), silky texture, poured through foam.
- Cappuccino: Equal parts espresso, steamed milk, and thick foam (3:3:3 ratio).
- Flat White: Very thin microfoam, ristretto shots, full-cream whole milk standard.
- Macchiato (Starbucks-style): Built in cup, milk poured first, then espresso.

2.4 MILK ALTERNATIVES
Temperature guidelines apply equally to all milk alternatives:
- Oat milk: Steams well, slightly sweeter, excellent foam.
- Coconut milk: Use Solo coconut milk beverage — does not foam as densely.
- Almond milk: Foams lightly — do not over-aerate.
- Soy milk: Steams similarly to dairy but can break at very high temperatures.
- Lactose-free (Fairlife): Steams identically to whole milk.

================================================================================
SECTION 3: FRAPPUCCINO BASE RECIPES
================================================================================

3.1 COFFEE FRAPPUCCINO BASE
Ingredients per Grande (16 oz):
- 2 pumps Frappuccino Roast (coffee base)
- 2 pumps Frappuccino syrup (sweetener)
- Whole milk to fill line 1 on pitcher
- Ice to fill line 2
Blend: 6 seconds on high. Should be thick, consistent, with no large ice chunks.

3.2 CREME FRAPPUCCINO BASE (NO COFFEE)
Ingredients per Grande:
- 2 pumps Frappuccino syrup
- 2 pumps flavoring syrup of choice
- Whole milk to fill line 1
- Ice to fill line 2
Blend: Same as coffee base. Used for Vanilla Bean, Strawberry, Matcha Cremes.

3.3 BLENDING STANDARDS
- Blender must be clean between every beverage.
- Never fill above MAX line — lid will not seal safely.
- Use the Vitamix preset "Frappuccino" where available.
- If blend is too thin: add 2 scoops of ice and blend again.
- If blend is too thick: add 1 oz of milk and blend for 3 seconds.
- Topped with whipped cream unless customer requests "no whip."

================================================================================
SECTION 4: CUSTOMIZATION GUIDELINES
================================================================================

4.1 SYRUP PUMPS BY SIZE
Standard syrup pumps (can be increased or decreased per customer request):
- Tall: 3 pumps
- Grande: 4 pumps
- Venti hot: 5 pumps
- Venti iced: 6 pumps
Extra syrup (1 additional pump) incurs an upcharge. Reduced sugar requests (fewer pumps)
are accommodated at no charge.

4.2 SAUCE VERSUS SYRUP
- Syrups: Thin, clear or translucent, pump into cup before milk (e.g., vanilla, caramel).
- Sauces: Thick, drizzle on top or in cup (e.g., mocha sauce, caramel sauce, white mocha).
Never substitute sauce for syrup quantities — they have different sugar concentrations.

4.3 MILK SUBSTITUTIONS
All milk alternatives available at a standard upcharge (typically $0.70-$0.80).
The following substitutions have no upcharge:
- Removing milk entirely (bone dry cappuccino, etc.)
- Substituting water for milk in Americano-style drinks.

4.4 ESPRESSO MODIFICATIONS
- Upside down: Pour espresso over milk instead of under — changes flavor profile.
- Blonde roast: Available as substitution for standard espresso at no charge.
- Decaf: Available but takes longer — set customer expectations appropriately.
- Half-caf: One regular shot, one decaf shot per double shot standard.

================================================================================
SECTION 5: SEASONAL DRINK PROTOCOLS
================================================================================

5.1 LAUNCH PROCEDURES
Seasonal beverages launch on the announced date at store opening.
Partners must complete seasonal training module in My Starbucks before launch day.
All seasonal syrups and sauces must be available before the first customer enters.

5.2 PUMPKIN SPICE LATTE (PSL)
Standard recipe (Grande):
- 3 pumps pumpkin sauce
- 2 shots espresso
- 2% milk steamed to 150°F
- Whipped cream and pumpkin spice topping
PSL sauce is seasonal — notify manager when inventory drops below 3 bottles.

5.3 PEPPERMINT MOCHA
Standard recipe (Grande):
- 3 pumps mocha sauce
- 2 pumps peppermint syrup
- 2 shots espresso
- 2% milk steamed to 150°F
- Whipped cream and mocha drizzle

5.4 MATCHA LATTE
Standard recipe (Grande):
- 2 scoops matcha powder
- 4 oz hot water (to dissolve matcha)
- Milk of choice steamed to 150°F
Note: Matcha powder must be fully dissolved before adding milk — never add directly.

================================================================================
SECTION 6: QUALITY CONTROL CHECKS
================================================================================

6.1 BEFORE SERVING
Every beverage must pass a visual quality check before handoff:
- Correct cup size selected.
- Cup labeled with correct customer name and customizations.
- No spills or drips on the outside of the cup.
- Lid properly sealed.
- Whipped cream not collapsed (if applicable).
- Correct sleeve applied for hot beverages over 140°F.

6.2 TASTING STANDARD
Partners are encouraged (not required) to taste test a sip of new seasonal drinks
during training to understand the flavor profile and confidently describe it to customers.

6.3 REMAKE POLICY
Any beverage that does not meet quality standards must be remade — no exceptions.
Substandard beverages should never be served hoping the customer won't notice.
Waste the original, log it in the waste tracker, and prepare again correctly.
The cost of a remade drink is far lower than the cost of a dissatisfied customer.
