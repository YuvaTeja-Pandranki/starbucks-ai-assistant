Version 2.7 | Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Confidential — For Store Manager and Shift Supervisor Use Only

SECTION 1: DAILY OPENING CHECKLIST
================================================================================

All opening duties must be completed by the Opening Shift Supervisor no later
than 30 minutes before store opening. Sign off each task in the Daily Log.

1.1 PRE-OPEN SAFETY & SECURITY (60 min before open)
    [ ] Disarm alarm system and verify no overnight incidents
    [ ] Inspect exterior: clear entrance, no hazards, signage in place
    [ ] Check back-of-house for any pest activity or water damage
    [ ] Verify safe balance matches previous closing report
    [ ] Confirm all staff have clocked in before open

1.2 EQUIPMENT STARTUP (45 min before open)
    [ ] Power on all espresso machines — run purge cycle (2x per machine)
    [ ] Calibrate grinders: check shot time (target 25–30 seconds for espresso)
    [ ] Power on blenders, warming ovens, and pastry cases
    [ ] Run POS terminal self-test on all registers
    [ ] Test mobile order queue connectivity
    [ ] Verify receipt paper loaded in all terminals

1.3 PRODUCT & INVENTORY SETUP (30 min before open)
    [ ] Pull daily dairy order from walk-in cooler; check expiration dates
    [ ] Stock condiment bar: sugars, stirrers, napkins, sleeves, stoppers
    [ ] Fill syrup pumps — check all bottles against inventory sheet
    [ ] Load pastry case with morning items; label with freshness times
    [ ] Verify ready-to-drink case is stocked and refrigerated
    [ ] Pull inventory counts for CRITICAL and LOW items (see Section 2)

1.4 CLEANLINESS & COMPLIANCE
    [ ] Sanitize all food contact surfaces with approved solution
    [ ] Verify handwashing station is stocked (soap, paper towels)
    [ ] Confirm all team members wearing proper uniform and PPE
    [ ] Check bathroom cleanliness and restock supplies
    [ ] Ensure allergen information cards are current and visible

1.5 OPEN STORE
    [ ] Unlock front door at scheduled open time
    [ ] Switch CLOSED sign to OPEN
    [ ] Post daily specials board
    [ ] Confirm Drive-Thru window is operational (if applicable)
    [ ] Notify manager on duty that store is open via Operations app

SECTION 2: INVENTORY MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

2.1 INVENTORY STATUS DEFINITIONS

    OK       — Stock level is above reorder threshold. No immediate action.
    LOW      — Stock is below reorder threshold but store can operate normally
               for the current day. Action required within 24 hours.
    CRITICAL — Stock level is severely depleted. Immediate risk to service
               continuity. Emergency action required same day.

2.2 LOW STATUS — ORDER WITHIN 24 HOURS
    When any item reaches LOW status:
    - Shift supervisor must flag the item in the Inventory Management System
    - Store Manager must place a standard replenishment order by end of business
    - Notify team to conserve usage where possible (e.g., portion control)
    - If LOW status is detected at open, order must be placed before 10:00 AM
    - Document in the Daily Inventory Log

2.3 CRITICAL STATUS — SAME-DAY EMERGENCY ORDER
    When any item reaches CRITICAL status:
    - Store Manager must be notified immediately regardless of shift
    - Emergency order must be placed within 1 hour of CRITICAL status detection
    - Contact Starbucks Supply Chain hotline: 1-800-SBX-SUPPLY
    - If item is beverage-critical (e.g., espresso, milk), evaluate whether
      affected menu items should be temporarily removed from the menu board
    - Notify District Manager if item unavailability will affect service for
      more than 2 hours
    - Document incident in Operations Log with timestamps

2.4 DAILY INVENTORY COUNTS
    - Opening supervisor conducts a spot check of top 10 high-velocity items
    - Full inventory count conducted every Tuesday and Friday at store close
    - Variance reports submitted to District Manager if shrinkage > 3%
    - Dairy products: count every morning before open; expiration check required

SECTION 3: CUSTOMER ESCALATION TIERS
================================================================================

3.1 TIER 1 — FRONTLINE RESOLUTION (Barista / Cashier)
    Scope: Minor complaints, simple service recovery
    Examples:
    - Drink made incorrectly — offer immediate remake
    - Wait time complaint — apologize, provide status update
    - Missing condiment or sleeve — resolve on the spot
    Action: Resolve immediately without escalation. No documentation required
    unless customer requests refund. Log in Daily Feedback Tracker.

3.2 TIER 2 — SHIFT SUPERVISOR INVOLVEMENT
    Scope: Moderate complaints requiring judgment or minor compensation
    Examples:
    - Refund request under $50
    - Repeated order errors for same customer
    - Customer threatening to leave a negative review
    - Minor altercation between customers
    Action: Shift Supervisor takes over interaction. Assess situation, offer
    remedy (refund, replacement, goodwill coupon). Document in incident log.
    Notify Store Manager if not on site.

3.3 TIER 3 — STORE MANAGER + DISTRICT ESCALATION
    Scope: High-impact complaints, safety concerns, or reputational risk
    Examples:
    - Refund request $50–$100 (District notification required)
    - Refund request above $100 (District approval required)
    - Allegation of food contamination or allergic reaction
    - Customer threatens legal action
    - Social media threat or media presence
    - Physical altercation or safety threat
    Action: Store Manager handles directly. Contact District Manager within
    1 hour. Preserve all transaction records. Do not offer unilateral
    settlements above $100 without District Manager approval. File incident
    report within 2 hours.

SECTION 4: PEAK HOUR PROTOCOLS
================================================================================

4.1 MORNING RUSH — 7:00 AM TO 9:00 AM

    Staffing: Minimum 4 partners on floor; dedicated drive-thru team if
    applicable (2 minimum for drive-thru stores)

    Bar Setup:
    - All espresso machines active with trained baristas
    - Pre-batch cold brew and iced tea; fill to capacity before 6:45 AM
    - Mobile order station staffed separately from walk-in counter
    - Condiment bar restocked at 6:50 AM; monitor every 20 minutes

    Queue Management:
    - Activate second register when line exceeds 4 customers
    - Supervisor floats between bar and register to prevent bottlenecks
    - Target wait time: under 3 minutes from order to handoff
    - Communicate delays proactively: "Your order will be ready in about
      [X] minutes" if queue exceeds 5 mobile orders

    Communication:
    - Barista calls customer names clearly; verify name on cup before handoff
    - Use "order ahead" signage to direct mobile customers to pickup shelf
    - Do not process cash-only transactions at peak; redirect to secondary lane

4.2 AFTERNOON RUSH — 12:00 PM TO 2:00 PM

    Staffing: Minimum 3 partners; 1 dedicated to Frappuccino/cold blended

    Bar Setup:
    - Stock cold cups, Frappuccino bases, and whip cream before noon
    - Restock syrups used heavily in AM; check Vanilla, Caramel, Hazelnut
    - Refresh food case with afternoon items (sandwiches, protein boxes)

    Queue Management:
    - Monitor mobile order queue — surge common from office lunch breaks
    - Proactively communicate estimated times for large blended orders
    - Drive-thru: supervisor monitors order accuracy rate during peak window

    After Rush:
    - Conduct equipment cleaning cycle at 2:15 PM
    - Log any inventory depletions in system for afternoon reorder review
    - Debrief shift: note any recurring errors or customer feedback themes

SECTION 5: STAFF MANAGEMENT RULES
================================================================================

5.1 SCHEDULING & ATTENDANCE
    - All schedule changes must be submitted 72 hours in advance via Workforce app
    - No-call, no-show: Manager must attempt contact within 30 minutes of shift start
    - Three unexcused absences in 90 days triggers formal performance review
    - Overtime must be pre-approved by Store Manager; District approval for >10 hrs/week

5.2 TRAINING & CERTIFICATION
    - All new partners must complete Coffee Passport within first 30 days
    - Barista certification required before solo bar shifts
    - Food Handler's Permit required in all locations; kept on file at store
    - Quarterly safety training mandatory for all staff

5.3 CONDUCT & PERFORMANCE
    - Cell phone use on the floor is prohibited except in designated break areas
    - Partners may not consume food or beverages on the floor (break room only)
    - Customer complaints naming a specific partner must be documented and
      reviewed within 48 hours by Store Manager
    - Positive customer feedback should be shared publicly at next team huddle

5.4 BREAK POLICY
    - Shifts 4–6 hours: one 10-minute paid break
    - Shifts over 6 hours: one 10-minute paid break + one 30-minute unpaid meal break
    - Break coverage must be arranged with supervisor before stepping away from post
    - Drive-thru and bar positions: staggered breaks only; never leave station unmanned

5.5 END OF SHIFT HANDOFF
    - Outgoing supervisor briefs incoming supervisor on: inventory flags, pending
      complaints, equipment issues, and any escalated customer situations
    - Cash drawers balanced and sealed before supervisor departure
    - All open incidents documented in shift handoff log
    - Equipment issues reported to maintenance system before close of shift
