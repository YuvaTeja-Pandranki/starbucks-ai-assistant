SECTION 1: TRANSACTION LOGGING REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

All financial transactions and AI-assisted decisions must be fully logged to
ensure auditability, fraud prevention, and regulatory compliance.

1.1 MANDATORY REFUND LOGGING
    Every refund transaction, regardless of amount, must be recorded in the
    Starbucks Transaction Management System (TMS) with the following fields:

    Required Fields:
    - transaction_id: Unique system-generated identifier
    - order_id: Original order reference number
    - customer_id: Loyalty ID or anonymized identifier (no raw PII in logs)
    - refund_amount: Amount refunded in USD
    - refund_reason_code: Standardized code from approved reason list
    - approval_level: SUPERVISOR / MANAGER / DISTRICT_MANAGER
    - approved_by: Employee ID of approving party
    - ai_recommendation: Flag indicating if AI system made a recommendation
    - hitl_required: Boolean — whether human-in-the-loop was triggered
    - hitl_decision: APPROVED / DENIED / MODIFIED (if HITL was triggered)
    - processed_timestamp: ISO 8601 datetime of processing
    - store_id: Store identifier

    Retention: All refund logs must be retained for a minimum of 7 years
    in compliance with financial record-keeping regulations.

1.2 REAL-TIME LOGGING
    Logs must be written to the TMS in real time at the moment of transaction
    processing. Batch logging is not permitted for refund events. Any logging
    failure must trigger an alert to the Store Manager immediately.

1.3 LOG INTEGRITY
    Transaction logs are immutable once written. Amendments must be filed as
    a separate correction record referencing the original transaction_id.
    Deletion of log records is strictly prohibited.

SECTION 2: DATA PRIVACY RULES
================================================================================

Starbucks is committed to protecting customer and employee data in accordance
with applicable privacy laws (CCPA, GDPR where applicable, and Starbucks
Global Privacy Policy).

2.1 NO PII IN CHAT LOGS OR AI INTERACTION RECORDS
    AI assistant interactions, chatbot transcripts, and system-generated
    conversation logs must NOT contain personally identifiable information (PII).

    Prohibited PII in logs:
    - Full customer name (use anonymized ID or first name + last initial only)
    - Email address, phone number, home address
    - Full credit card number, CVV, or expiration date
    - Date of birth
    - Government-issued ID numbers
    - Full loyalty account number (use masked format: SBX-XXXX-XXXX)

    Permitted in logs:
    - Anonymized customer ID (system-generated)
    - Order ID
    - Store ID
    - Timestamp
    - Transaction amount
    - Reason codes (standardized, non-descriptive)

2.2 DATA MINIMIZATION
    AI systems should collect and process only the minimum data necessary to
    complete the task. AI agents must not request or store customer data
    beyond what is required for the current interaction.

2.3 DATA RETENTION FOR AI LOGS
    AI interaction logs are retained for 90 days for quality assurance and
    model evaluation. After 90 days, logs are anonymized and retained for
    an additional 12 months for compliance auditing. Raw interaction data
    is purged after 90 days.

2.4 CUSTOMER DATA ACCESS REQUESTS
    If a customer requests access to or deletion of their data, the request
    must be routed to privacy@starbucks.internal within 24 hours. AI systems
    may not independently fulfill data access or deletion requests.

2.5 THIRD-PARTY DATA SHARING
    AI-processed customer data must not be shared with third-party vendors
    without explicit data processing agreements in place. District Manager
    approval required for any data export request.

SECTION 3: AI GOVERNANCE RULES
================================================================================

Starbucks uses AI-assisted tools to support store operations. The following
rules govern the acceptable use of AI in operational decisions.

3.1 AI IS ADVISORY ONLY
    All AI recommendations are advisory in nature. AI systems do not have
    authority to make final decisions on:
    - Refund approvals (any amount)
    - Staff disciplinary actions
    - Menu pricing or promotional decisions
    - Inventory emergency orders above standard thresholds
    - Customer escalations involving legal or safety concerns

    In all cases, a human operator (Shift Supervisor, Store Manager, or
    District Manager) must review and confirm the AI recommendation before
    any action is taken.

3.2 HUMAN-IN-THE-LOOP (HITL) — REQUIRED ABOVE $50
    Any AI-assisted refund recommendation involving an amount of $50.00 or
    greater must be explicitly reviewed and approved by a human operator
    before the refund is processed.

    HITL Process:
    a) AI system generates recommendation with justification
    b) System flags transaction for human review (HITL trigger)
    c) Store Manager or District Manager receives notification
    d) Human reviews: transaction history, refund policy, AI reasoning
    e) Human selects: APPROVE / DENY / MODIFY
    f) Decision and rationale logged in TMS
    g) Action executed only after human confirmation

    AI systems must surface the following to the human reviewer:
    - Order details and history
    - Refund eligibility assessment
    - Policy section applicable
    - Recommended action with confidence score
    - Flag if customer has prior refund history (frequency check)

3.3 AI CONFIDENCE THRESHOLDS
    AI systems must report a confidence score with every recommendation:
    - HIGH (>85%): AI recommendation displayed; human may accept or override
    - MEDIUM (60–85%): AI recommendation displayed with caution flag
    - LOW (<60%): AI surfaces options only; human must make independent decision

3.4 AI SYSTEM FAILURE PROTOCOL
    If the AI system is unavailable or produces an error:
    - Fall back to manual decision-making using printed policy guides
    - Do not delay customer resolution due to AI system unavailability
    - Log the AI system failure in the Operations Log with timestamp
    - Notify IT Support: itsupport@starbucks.internal within 1 hour

3.5 PROHIBITED AI USES
    AI tools must not be used for:
    - Facial recognition or biometric identification of customers or staff
    - Automated profiling of customers based on purchase history for
      differential service treatment
    - Autonomous communication with customers via email, SMS, or social media
    - Any decision that involves employment termination or formal discipline

SECTION 4: ACCESS CONTROL LEVELS
================================================================================

Access to operational systems, AI tools, and customer data is governed by
role-based access control (RBAC). Access is granted on a least-privilege basis.

4.1 STORE MANAGER
    Authorized Systems: POS, TMS (read/write), Inventory Management,
    Workforce Scheduling, Refund Portal (up to $100), AI Assistant (full access)
    Data Access: Store-level transaction data, staff schedules, inventory
    Restrictions: Cannot access data from other stores; cannot approve
    refunds above $100 without District Manager confirmation
    AI Permissions: Can approve AI refund recommendations up to $100

4.2 DISTRICT MANAGER
    Authorized Systems: All store-level systems (read/write) within district,
    Regional Reporting Dashboard, Refund Portal (unlimited), AI System Audit Logs
    Data Access: All stores in assigned district, aggregated performance metrics,
    AI recommendation history and HITL decision logs
    Restrictions: Cannot access data outside assigned district without
    Regional Manager authorization
    AI Permissions: Can approve all AI recommendations; can override AI
    confidence flags; authorized to request AI model evaluation reports

4.3 REGIONAL MANAGER / OPERATIONS
    Authorized Systems: All district-level systems (read-only), Regional
    Compliance Dashboard, Audit Trail system (full access)
    Data Access: All stores in region; cross-district comparison data;
    full AI system logs including model performance metrics
    Restrictions: Production system write access requires IT change management
    AI Permissions: Can initiate AI model review; can suspend AI features
    in specific stores or districts pending compliance review

4.4 SHIFT SUPERVISOR
    Authorized Systems: POS (limited), Refund Portal (up to $50), Inventory
    spot-check tool, AI Assistant (read-only recommendations)
    Data Access: Current shift transactions only
    Restrictions: No access to historical customer data beyond current shift;
    cannot approve HITL transactions
    AI Permissions: Can view AI recommendations; cannot approve HITL triggers

4.5 BARISTA / CASHIER
    Authorized Systems: POS (transaction entry only)
    Data Access: Current customer order in progress only
    Restrictions: No access to refund portal, inventory management, or
    AI systems
    AI Permissions: None

SECTION 5: AUDIT EVENTS TO TRACK
================================================================================

The following events must be captured in the centralized Audit Event Log
in real time. All events include: event_type, actor_id, store_id,
timestamp, and relevant entity IDs.

5.1 FINANCIAL AUDIT EVENTS
    - REFUND_INITIATED: Any refund request entered into system
    - REFUND_APPROVED: Final approval of refund transaction
    - REFUND_DENIED: Refund request rejected with reason code
    - REFUND_MODIFIED: Approved amount differs from requested amount
    - LARGE_REFUND_FLAGGED: Any refund >= $50 flagged for HITL
    - DUPLICATE_CHARGE_DETECTED: System or manual detection of double charge
    - CASH_VARIANCE_REPORTED: End-of-day cash count differs from POS total

5.2 AI SYSTEM AUDIT EVENTS
    - AI_RECOMMENDATION_GENERATED: AI produces a decision recommendation
    - HITL_TRIGGERED: Human review required (threshold exceeded)
    - HITL_APPROVED: Human approves AI recommendation
    - HITL_DENIED: Human rejects AI recommendation
    - HITL_MODIFIED: Human modifies AI recommendation before approving
    - AI_OVERRIDE: Human overrides AI recommendation with different decision
    - AI_SYSTEM_ERROR: AI system fails to produce recommendation
    - AI_LOW_CONFIDENCE: AI confidence score falls below 60%

5.3 ACCESS CONTROL AUDIT EVENTS
    - USER_LOGIN: Successful authentication to any operational system
    - USER_LOGIN_FAILED: Failed authentication attempt (alert after 3 failures)
    - PRIVILEGE_ESCALATION: User accesses system above their role level
    - DATA_EXPORT: Any export of customer or transaction data
    - ACCESS_DENIED: User attempts to access restricted resource
    - ROLE_CHANGE: User's access level is modified

5.4 OPERATIONAL AUDIT EVENTS
    - INVENTORY_CRITICAL_ALERT: Item reaches CRITICAL status
    - INVENTORY_EMERGENCY_ORDER: Emergency supply order placed
    - ESCALATION_TIER3_OPENED: Tier 3 customer escalation initiated
    - INCIDENT_REPORT_FILED: Formal incident report submitted
    - POLICY_EXCEPTION_GRANTED: Manager grants exception to standard policy

5.5 AUDIT LOG REQUIREMENTS
    - Logs must be tamper-evident (cryptographic hash chain preferred)
    - Retention: 7 years minimum for financial events; 2 years for system events
    - Access to audit logs restricted to District Manager and above
    - Automated anomaly detection must run daily on audit log patterns
    - Monthly audit summary report submitted to Regional Compliance Officer
